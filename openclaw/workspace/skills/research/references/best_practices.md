# Research Best Practices

## Planning Your Research

### 1. Define Clear Objectives
- Start with specific research questions
- What exactly do you need to know?
- How will you use the information?
- What constitutes "enough" research?

### 2. Scope Your Research
- Set boundaries: time period, geographic focus, source types
- Determine depth vs. breadth trade-offs
- Identify what's out of scope

### 3. Choose Methodology
- Qualitative vs. quantitative approaches
- Primary vs. secondary sources
- Experimental vs. observational

## Source Evaluation

### 1. Authority and Credibility
- Who created the source? What are their qualifications?
- Is the source peer-reviewed or editorially reviewed?
- What is the reputation of the publication/platform?

### 2. Accuracy and Reliability
- Can information be verified through other sources?
- Are claims supported by evidence?
- Are there citations or references to original research?

### 3. Objectivity and Bias
- What is the purpose of the source? (Inform, persuade, sell, entertain)
- Are multiple viewpoints presented?
- Is language neutral or emotionally charged?
- Who funded the research or content creation?

### 4. Currency and Relevance
- When was the source published or last updated?
- Is the information still current for your topic?
- Does it directly address your research question?

## Information Organization

### 1. Note-Taking Systems
- Use consistent formatting for notes
- Include source citations with every note
- Distinguish between direct quotes and paraphrasing
- Note your own thoughts and reactions separately

### 2. Digital Organization
- Use descriptive filenames and folder structures
- Tag sources by topic, type, and relevance
- Maintain a master bibliography or source list
- Use reference management tools when appropriate

### 3. Synthesis Techniques
- Create concept maps or mind maps
- Develop outlines before writing
- Use comparison charts for contrasting viewpoints
- Identify patterns, trends, and gaps in information

## Ethical Considerations

### 1. Academic Integrity
- Always cite your sources properly
- Never present others' work as your own
- Understand and follow citation conventions for your field
- When in doubt, cite the source

### 2. Respect for Intellectual Property
- Follow fair use guidelines for copyrighted material
- Seek permission when needed for substantial use
- Respect licenses and terms of use for digital content
- Give credit for ideas, not just direct quotes

### 3. Responsible Use of Information
- Consider potential impacts of sharing sensitive information
- Verify information before sharing it with others
- Be transparent about limitations and uncertainties in your findings
- Correct errors promptly when discovered

## Research Workflow Optimization

### 1. Iterative Approach
- Start broad, then narrow based on findings
- Let early results inform later search strategies
- Be willing to revise research questions as you learn
- Build in time for reflection and synthesis

### 2. Efficient Searching
- Use advanced search operators (quotes, AND/OR/NOT, site:, filetype:)
- Try synonyms and related terms
- Mine bibliographies of good sources for more references
- Set up alerts for ongoing topics

### 3. Managing Information Overload
- Set time limits for initial gathering phases
- Use skimming and scanning techniques effectively
- Develop quick evaluation heuristics
- Take regular breaks to maintain focus

### 4. Collaboration and Feedback
- Discuss findings with peers or mentors
- Seek feedback on your research approach
- Consider different perspectives on your topic
- Share preliminary findings for validation

## Specific to Available Tools

### YouTube Research
- Check multiple videos on same topic for consistency
- Look at video comments for additional resources or critiques
- Consider upload date and view count as quality indicators
- Transcripts may contain errors - verify key points

### NotebookLM Research
- Start with high-quality, diverse sources
- Use specific, focused queries rather than vague questions
- Take advantage of AI-generated summaries but verify claims
- Experiment with different artifact types for different purposes

### General Web Research
- Go beyond first page of search results
- Use specialized search engines for specific topics (scholar, news, etc.)
- Check domain authority (.edu, .gov, reputable .org)
- Use Wayback Machine for inaccessible or changed content

## Documentation and Reproducibility

### 1. Research Log
- Keep track of search terms used and when
- Note which sources were consulted and rejected
- Record any obstacles or dead ends encountered
- Document decisions about source inclusion/exclusion

### 2. Source Tracking
- Maintain a working bibliography from the start
- Include all necessary citation information
- Note access dates for online sources
- Use consistent citation format throughout

### 3. Process Documentation
- Describe your research methodology
- Note any tools or techniques used
- Record deviations from original plan
- Explain how you synthesized information

## Quality Assurance

### 1. Triangulation
- Seek confirmation from multiple independent sources
- Look for convergence of evidence from different methodologies
- Be wary of single-source claims, especially surprising ones

### 2. Peer Review
- Have others check your work for obvious errors
- Consider alternative interpretations of your findings
- Be open to revising conclusions based on feedback

### 3. Self-Check
- Review your work for logical consistency
- Check that conclusions follow from evidence presented
- Verify that you haven't ignored contradictory evidence
- Ensure proper attribution throughout

## Adapting to Different Research Types

### 1. Fact-Finding Research
- Prioritize authoritative sources
- Cross-check facts across multiple references
- Note any uncertainties or disputed information
- Focus on accuracy and precision

### 2. Exploratory Research
- Cast a wide net initially
- Follow interesting leads as they emerge
- Be open to unexpected connections
- Document your journey of discovery

### 3. Comparative Research
- Establish clear comparison criteria
- Ensure sources are comparable in quality and type
- Look for both similarities and differences
- Consider context when making comparisons

### 4. Problem-Solving Research
- Define the problem clearly before searching
- Look for proven solutions and best practices
- Consider implementation challenges and trade-offs
- Seek both theoretical and practical guidance
